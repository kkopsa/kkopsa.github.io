Kevin Kopsa

Software Developer

In January 2012, I started my career as a software developer when I took my first programming class at BYU-Idaho. I realized quickly that I liked high-level programming languages.

Since then, I have grown to love programming and software development even more.

Throughout my education, I have had the opportunity to hone my skills in various capacities. I worked in software quality assurance as a tester for Surge™, a custom software development and consulting company. I gained experience in software development methodology as well as technical knowledge of testing web and mobile applications.

Afterwards I continued my education for a few more semesters and later became a mobile application developer for the same company I previously worked at. I gained experience with both Android and iOS mobile platforms.

I worked as a web developer at FamilySearch. I developed web applications using Java, AngularJS, PostgresQL, Hibernate, Maven, and various other technologies along the way.

I am passionate about mobile and web technologies and applications and the potential they have to do good throughout the world. I love the idea of helping everyone, especially developing countries utilize this technology that has been placed in the hands of billions around the globe.

It is my dream to further mobile and web technologies as a means to help developing countries throughout the world.
